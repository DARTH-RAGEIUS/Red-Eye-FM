BUILDING A SUCCESSFUL MUSIC CAREER.

By Vivian Kagwiria | Red Eye FM

Music industry consists of companies and individuals that earn money by creating new songs and pieces and selling live concerts and shows, audio and video recordings, compositions and sheet music and the organization and associations that aid and represent music creators.

Making it the music industry is not as easy as ABC. Between rejection, lack of funds and personal hardships, few musicians ever make it big. Now, how do you build a successful career? Easy, begin writing, whether writing lyrics or strumming away on the guitar, you should always be playing. Second, invest in quality Audio/visual equipments. There is nothing more heartbreaking than a screeching microphone or amp. Without the right sound equipment, you wind up turning off more fans than you win over. Finding an instrument and equipment distributor is a good place to start.

Moving on, know the industry. Signing with a major record label is a major deal but you have to start small. As the saying goes, Rome wasn't built in a day. Know the type of music you play and your target audience. The challenging thing about a music career is not knowing where it will take you but at the same time that is what makes it exciting. Climbing up-to the top!

Fourthly, be in control. At the beginning, you’ll struggle the most. As a result you may feel the urge to sign a deal with the first promoter or producer you encounter. Do not be hasty in your decisions. Book your own shows wherever it’s best for you. Write your own albums and produce them on your own accord. As your career grows, the right manager, promoters and producers will come along. Again build connections. You don’t want to burn bridges especially with powerful people also you don’t have to please everybody but you don’t want to do anything that can give you a bad reputation.

Lastly, play because of the music. The most important thing you can do as a musician is play for the music and it’s art. You don’t want a career in music for the fame and money. You got into music once upon a time because of passion for it. On the other hand, there are a million and one reasons artist fail but the #1 top reason they fail is simple: It all boils down to not having the right mindset. Understanding that what you think is what determines your course and your music career. But that’s all for another day. Having all this in mind, a successful music career awaits!

'At the beginning, you’ll struggle the most. As a result you may feel the urge to sign a deal with the first promoter or producer you encounter. Do not be hasty in your decisions. Book your own shows wherever it’s best for you.'
Vivian Kagwiria
